Section 1 · ~2 minutes

The Brief and the User

Start here. Your audience cannot evaluate your design without knowing who it is for.

The test: Can you introduce your user in under 60 seconds without looking at your Figma file? If not, you do not know your persona well enough yet. Keep practising until you can.
Section 2 · ~2 minutes

Research and Competitor Analysis

What did you find? What gap does your design address?

Section 3 · ~3 minutes · Part A

Design Decisions

Walk through your hi-fi mockup. For each decision, explain WHY — not what you like, but what justifies the choice.

Colour palette

Why these colours? Reference your persona, the category emotional tone, or a competitor contrast gap.

Typography

Why this font pairing? How does it communicate your brand personality? Reference readability for your persona.

Visual hierarchy

What does the eye land on first? Is that your primary CTA? Reference the hook / explainer / descriptor model.

Continue on the next slide: Navigation · Hero section · Layout and spacing
Section 3 · ~3 minutes · Part B

Design Decisions continued

Every sentence must connect a visual decision to the brief, the persona, or a measurable standard.

Navigation

What pattern did you choose and why? Reference category conventions and your persona's expected behaviour.

Hero section

What is the primary CTA and what makes it visible? What image treatment did you choose and why?

Layout and spacing

How does your 8px grid create visual rhythm? Reference CRAP principles — alignment and proximity.

The rule: If you catch yourself saying 'I chose this because I like it' — stop. Ask what research or principle supports that choice. If there is none, reconsider the decision.
Section 4 · ~2 minutes

What You Would Do Differently

Self-reflection is part of the grade. This is not weakness — it is design thinking.

How to frame it well: 'The contrast ratio on my secondary text fails WCAG AA — I would darken the grey from #9CA3AF to #4B5563.' Specific, self-aware, actionable. Not: 'I would make it better.'
